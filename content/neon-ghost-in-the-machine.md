
Every time the page refreshed, a faint chromatic aberration bled into the edges of the Dela Gothic text, making the letters look like they were vibrating. It was a "glitch" he’d intentionally coded to mimic his old, failing CRT monitor. 

He hovered his mouse over the pixel-art heart, and for a split second, the neon cyan drop-shadow seemed to glow brighter. It was as if the archive wasn't just storing data, but capturing the ghost of a decade that refused to be forgotten.

He sometimes wondered if the code had gained a mind of its own. Between the scanlines and the refresh cycles, a presence lingered—a warmth that shouldn't exist in a machine, but did.
