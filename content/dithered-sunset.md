
Aya stared at the monitor, watching the ordered dithering transform a simple sunset into a sea of tiny, purposeful dots. The UI was a chaotic masterpiece of pink and neon accents and heavy black borders that mirrored the Dela Gothic headers of her favorite BBS. 

She reached out to the yellow star sticker stuck to the corner of the bezel, its "95" logo tilted at a jaunty angle. In this low-resolution world, every chromatic aberration on the screen felt like a fragment of a memory she wasn’t ready to delete.

Outside the window, the real Tokyo sunset was fading into a hazy grey, but here on the screen, the colors remained vibrant and fixed—a digital sanctuary where time was measured in refresh rates and scanlines.
