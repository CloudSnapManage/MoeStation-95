
It wasn’t a letter, but it was just as fragile. He handed her a pastel pink floppy disk, the white label missing its usual title. Instead, in shaky blue ballpoint ink, it just said: "Don't open until you're home." 

When she finally slid it into her drive, the holographic shutter flashed like a secret. The screen didn't show files or folders—just a single, fullscreen 8-bit red heart pulsing against a dithered black background, flickering in time with her own heartbeat.

There were no words, only the rhythm of the data and the glow of the heart. It was the most honest thing anyone had ever said to her, written in a language only they understood.
