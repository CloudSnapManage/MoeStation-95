
At Sato-san's stall, you don't order from a menu. You sit down, pay your credits, and wait.

He watches the way the steam rises from the giant vat of pork bone broth. He studies the patterns of the chili oil as it floats on the surface. Today, he served me a bowl with extra nori, arranged in a jagged, uneven line.

"The grid will fail in the East," he said, not looking up from his chopping block. "The lights will go out, and the shadows will finally have their say. Take the long way home, child."

I laughed it off until I reached the station. The East-bound line was dark. A massive power surge had fried the mag-lev rails. I walked home under the pale moonlight, the taste of miso still lingering on my tongue, feeling the weight of a future I wasn't prepared for.
